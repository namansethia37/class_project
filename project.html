Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Agra Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1856. The fort is known for its stunning architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves are a series of Buddhist cave monuments located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra. These caves were carved out of volcanic rock and feature beautiful murals and sculptures that depict the life of Buddha.
